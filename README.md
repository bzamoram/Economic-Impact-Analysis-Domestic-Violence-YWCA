## üìã Project Overview

This data analytics project evaluates the **economic impact of domestic violence** in Cass County, North Dakota, and Clay County, Minnesota, while quantifying the **value of intervention services** provided by the YWCA. Using data-driven analysis, this study demonstrates how YWCA's programs mitigate community costs and support strategic decision-making for resource allocation [file:1].

### Key Findings
- **$31.5 million**: Annual economic burden of domestic violence (medical, mental health, lost productivity)
- **$79.45 million**: Annual taxpayer cost of domestic violence-related crimes
- **$3.6 million**: Cost savings generated by YWCA interventions in 2023
- **11.4%**: Reduction in domestic violence burden attributable to YWCA programs
- **2,162**: Victimizations prevented by YWCA emergency shelter services

## üî¨ Methodology

### Data Collection & Processing
1. **Crime Statistics**: Compiled 431 criminal offenses and 4,757 dispatch calls related to domestic violence
2. **Population Analysis**: Analyzed 91,039 adult females in Cass/Clay Counties
3. **Cost Estimation**: Applied national prevalence rates (116 per 1,000) to regional demographics
4. **Inflation Adjustment**: Adjusted 2007 crime cost data to 2023 values using 1.47 multiplier 

### Analysis Framework
The project examined three primary cost categories:
- **Medical Costs**: Emergency room visits, ambulance transport, inpatient stays, medical visits
- **Mental Health Costs**: Therapy sessions, counseling services, out-of-pocket expenses
- **Productivity Losses**: Lost workdays, wage impacts, economic productivity

## üí° Recommendations

Based on the analysis, five key recommendations emerged:

1. **Increased Funding for YWCA Programs**: Expand nurse-led care, emergency shelter capacity, and mental health services to serve more survivors
2. **Enhanced Data Collection Practices**: Develop standardized reporting systems with healthcare providers and law enforcement
3. **Public-Private Stakeholder Collaboration**: Establish formal partnerships with hospitals, insurers, and local governments
4. **Prevention-Focused Community Education**: Fund educational campaigns in schools, workplaces, and at-risk communities
5. **Leveraging Economic Impact Data**: Use cost-benefit findings in grant applications and policy advocacy [file:1]

## üîç Strengths & Limitations

### Strengths
- **Quantitative Rigor**: Used validated cost calculators with inflation adjustments
- **Multi-Source Integration**: Combined crime data, healthcare records, and organizational reports
- **Regional Relevance**: Tailored analysis to Cass/Clay Counties' specific demographics
- **Actionable Insights**: Provided concrete recommendations grounded in data [file:1]

### Limitations
- **National Prevalence Assumptions**: Applied national DV rates (may not perfectly reflect local conditions)
- **Scope Constraints**: Focused primarily on adult female survivors
- **Unquantified Intangible Costs**: Did not account for emotional trauma or intergenerational effects
- **Baseline Metrics**: Limited longitudinal data for trend analysis [file:1]

*This project demonstrates the power of data analytics in social impact assessment and evidence-based advocacy for community health initiatives.*
